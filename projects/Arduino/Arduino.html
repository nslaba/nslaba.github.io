// Early Interests

Where It Started

Before I worked professionally with microcontrollers, there was this moment of curiosity: soldering my first LED matrix and watching code become something physical.

The Spark

My academic background was predominantly software-focused, but I kept finding myself drawn to the hardware side of things. There's something satisfying about code that makes physical things happen.

This LED matrix was one of my first projects outside the classroom. Hand-soldering all the connections for data and current, then programming it to display a scrolling sign. Simple by professional standards, but it was the moment I realized this was what I wanted to do.

From Hobby to Career

That curiosity led me to where I am now: working professionally with embedded systems and microcontrollers. What started as weekend Arduino projects evolved into a career building firmware and working with hardware at a much deeper level.

Looking back at this project, the code was rough and the soldering could have been cleaner. But it represents something important: the willingness to learn by doing, even when you don't know what you're doing yet.
