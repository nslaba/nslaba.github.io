Hello, I'm Natalie — artist & engineer

I'm a Computer Science and Visual Arts graduate from the University of Victoria, based in British Columbia, Canada. I believe the most interesting work happens at the intersection of technical rigor and creative expression.

On the technical side, I'm drawn to graphics programming, interactive experiences, and educational technology—finding ways to make abstract concepts tangible and explorable. On the artistic side, I work primarily in oil and acrylic, exploring texture, light, and the quiet beauty of careful observation.

When I'm not at a computer or easel, you'll likely find me rock climbing, skiing, or exploring the mountains of the Pacific Northwest.

What I'm excited about

Educational Technology

Building interactive experiences that help people understand complex concepts through exploration and play. Math shouldn't feel abstract—it should feel tangible.

Graphics Programming

From ray tracers to real-time shaders, I love understanding how images are created computationally and pushing the boundaries of what's possible.

Visual Art

Oil painting, drawing, and mixed media. There's something meditative about the slow, deliberate process of observing and translating that onto canvas.
