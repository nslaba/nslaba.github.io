Engineer, artist, polyglot

I build embedded systems by day and paint by night. Currently a software engineer at Electronic Theatre Controls, working on real-time firmware under strict memory and timing constraints. Previously at Visual Concepts (2K Games), where I developed level-of-detail techniques for next-gen game engines.

My work sits at the intersection of technical rigor and creative exploration. I studied Computer Science and Visual Arts at the University of Victoria, and spent a semester doing AI research on simulating human movement through egocentric perception models. I use visual art as a language for ideas that exist between or beyond words.

Growing up across continents shaped how I think about communication, identity, and understanding. I speak six languages fluently, and that experience has made me endlessly curious about how we construct meaning and connect across difference.

This site exists for a few reasons. Partly as a living portfolio - a place to gather the scattered threads of what I've built over the years. Partly as a map for myself, tracing the territories I've wandered through and looking for where they might one day converge. And partly so that when friends and family ask what I mean by "graphics," I can finally point them somewhere instead of watching their eyes glaze over.

I've pulled myself in different directions over the years - logical, creative, cultural - each path driven by curiosity about what it means to experience life from another angle. What I keep coming back to is this: I care about people. About connection. About the way understanding something new can shift how you see everything else. Education, in particular, draws me - the kind that makes complex ideas click, that turns confusion into clarity. If I can eventually weave these scattered skills into something that gives back, that's the through-line I'm looking for.

I enjoy being outside, whether skiing or climbing.
